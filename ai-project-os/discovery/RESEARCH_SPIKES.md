# Research Spikes

## Status

Draft for planning.

## Purpose

This document lists short bounded investigations that may be needed before or during early implementation.

## Candidate spike RS-001

### Title

Evaluate first-problem-family candidates for v1.

### Goal

Compare 2 to 4 candidate v1 problem families and rank them by usefulness, tractability, benchmark availability, and architectural value.

### Output

Decision memo recommending the first target family.

## Candidate spike RS-002

### Title

Assess first reusable geometry abstraction.

### Goal

Determine whether flare-law-centered geometry abstraction is the correct early reusable core for horn and waveguide workflows.

### Output

Short contract proposal for geometry and flare interfaces.

## Candidate spike RS-003

### Title

Assess first solver-path strategy.

### Goal

Determine which first solver path best balances practical value, complexity, and validation potential.

### Output

Recommended first solver path with deferred alternatives.

## Candidate spike RS-004

### Title

Benchmark source survey.

### Goal

Identify candidate analytical, published, trusted-software, or measured references suitable for early benchmark use.

### Output

Prioritized benchmark list.

## Spike rules

- each spike must have a bounded question,
- each spike must end in a decision or explicit unresolved status,
- spikes are not production coding tasks,
- spike results must feed scope or architecture decisions.
